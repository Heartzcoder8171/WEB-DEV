About

Goa is a state located on the western coast of India, known for its rich history, cultural heritage, and natural beauty. It is the smallest state in India by area and fourth-smallest by population. History Goa was a Portuguese colony for about 456 years until it was annexed by India in 1961. The state has a unique mix of Indian and Portuguese cultures and architecture. Capital and Largest City The capital of Goa is Panaji, and the largest city is Vasco da Gama. Districts Goa is divided into two districts: North Goa and South Goa, with headquarters in Panjim and Margao respectively.
